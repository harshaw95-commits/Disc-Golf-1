Camera-assisted disc profiling

Disc Flight Lab

Use your camera, a known-size reference object, and quick image annotations to estimate rim diameter, dome shape, and parting line height so you can better predict stability and flight.

How it works

  1. Start the rear camera and frame the disc with a ruler or card of known width.
  2. Capture a top view to measure diameter and rim width.
  3. Capture a side profile to measure dome height and parting line height.
  4. Review the calculated profile and a heuristic stability summary.
